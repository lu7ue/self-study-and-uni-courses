Coding EXP

My venture into the world of programming has been a captivating expedition.

Not too long ago, I took the initiative to immerse myself in the realm of coding independently. It all began last year when I embarked on a learning journey with HTML and dabbled in CSS through the fantastic online resource, freeCodeCamp. It was akin to sculpting the fundamental structure and aesthetics of a website, an experience that felt like crafting a digital poster for an enthralling event.

My curiosity didn't stop there; it led me to Python, a remarkably versatile programming language. I sought knowledge through YouTube tutorials, which enabled me to grasp the foundational concepts of this language.

When it comes to writing and executing my code, I rely on Visual Studio Code. For organizing my learning materials and documenting my discoveries, I've found Notion to be an invaluable tool. It serves as my digital notebook, where I meticulously record the intriguing aspects of my coding journey.

It's worth mentioning that I don't have every detail memorized, but that's perfectly fine. My journey into programming is fueled by genuine passion and curiosity.

Interestingly, I've observed that programming shares similarities with the process of creating art. It's like painting, but instead of brushes and colors, you craft your canvas with pixels and logic. Both endeavors offer unique avenues for expressing creativity and ingenuity.

This journey into programming began as a personal interest, much like the exploration of a new and exhilarating art form.
